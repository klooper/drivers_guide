2. Fares and Payments

2.1. How to Tell if a Rider is Cash or eWallet

Open the rider's trip card in Spare Driver. The payment method is displayed on the trip detail screen. Cash riders must pay with exact change. eWallet riders have their fare deducted automatically — do not collect cash from them unless they are making a top-off payment.

2.2. Collecting Fares

  • Passengers must have exact change. The standard fare is $3.00.
  • If a passenger does not have exact change, they may make a donation of any amount.
  • Document all donations on the fare box ticket.

2.3. eWallet Top-Offs

If a rider gives you cash or a check to add to their eWallet balance:

  1. Accept the payment and note the amount.
  2. Record it on the fare box ticket.
  3. Report it to dispatch so the credit can be applied.

Do not attempt to apply the top-off yourself in Spare.

2.4. Companions and Caregivers

2.5. Children

Riders under the age of 11 ride free when accompanied by a paying adult.

4. Mobility Assistance and Accommodations

4.1. Scope of Driver Assistance

Drivers are expected to provide reasonable assistance to riders with limited mobility. This includes:

  • Deploying and stowing the lift or ramp.
  • Assisting riders to and from their seat.
  • Securing wheelchairs and mobility devices per securement procedures.

Drivers are not expected to provide attendant-level services. This includes personal care, medical assistance, or physical support beyond what is needed to safely board and exit the vehicle. If a rider requires attendant-level care, they should travel with a personal care attendant.

5. Forms and Reporting

5.1. Accidents

Any accident, no matter how minor, must be reported to management immediately. Complete an accident report before the end of your shift.

The manager on duty uses the decision tree below to determine whether drug and alcohol testing is required under FTA authority:

flowchart TD A([ACCIDENT]) --> B["Did the accident involve a revenue service vehicle,\nwhether in or out of revenue service?"] B -->|No| NT1["No test required — document"] B -->|Yes| C["Was the occurrence associated with\nthe manner of operation?"] C -->|No| NT1 C -->|Yes| D["Was there a fatality?"] D -->|Yes| TASAP1["Test ASAP\nDriver + any SS employee\nwho could have contributed\n "] D -->|No| E["Was anyone immediately transported\nto a medical treatment facility?"] E -->|No| H["Was there disabling damage\nto any vehicle?"] E -->|Yes| F["Can the operator's performance be completely\ndiscounted as a contributing factor?"] F -->|Yes| NT2["No test required — document"] F -->|No| TASAP2["Test ASAP\nDriver + any SS employee\nwho could have contributed"] H -->|Yes| F2["Can the operator's performance be completely\ndiscounted as a contributing factor?"] H -->|No| NT4["No test required — document"] F2 -->|Yes| NT5["No test required — document"] F2 -->|No| TASAP4["Test ASAP\nDriver + any SS employee\nwho could have contributed"]

SS — Safety-Sensitive Employee — any employee whose position requires a commercial driver's license or who performs safety-sensitive functions, including vehicle operators and dispatchers.

Disabling Damage — damage that precludes departure of a motor vehicle from the scene in its usual manner in daylight after simple repairs. Excludes: temporary roadside repairs, tire replacement without other damage, headlamp or tail light damage, or damage to signals/horn/wipers.

For your files: attach test results summary, order to test, Custody and Control Form (USDOT), and alcohol testing form (USDOT).

Timing requirements:

  • Alcohol test must be performed within 2 hours of the accident.
  • Drug test must be performed within 32 hours of the accident.

5.7. Fare Receipts

The fare box ticket is the primary record of all cash collected during your shift. Accuracy and legibility directly affect the reconciliation process — illegible or incomplete entries create errors that take significant time to resolve.

For each transaction, record:

  • Date — the correct date of service. This is critical for reconciliation.
  • Rider name — first initial and full last name at minimum (e.g., J. Smith). Write clearly.
  • Amount collected — the exact amount received.
  • Top-offs — write TO next to the entry to indicate an eWallet top-off payment.

If you make a mistake, draw a single line through the entry and initial it. Do not scribble out or obscure the original — the crossed-out entry must still be readable.

When your shift ends, see Fare Box Turnover for how to submit the ticket.

6. End of Shift

6.1. Post-Trip Inspection

Complete your post-trip inspection before signing out.

6.2. Fare Box Turnover

  • If money was collected: place the fare box ticket into the fare box and put it in the locker.
  • If no money was collected: place the ticket in the designated bowl. Put the box back in the stack.

6.3. Sign-Out

  • Make sure the bus has at least a half tank of fuel. See Fueling Procedures if needed.
  • Turn off the communication radio.
  • Sign out of Spare Driver.
  • Plug in the radio, tablet, and hang up the bus keys.

7. Fueling Procedures

7.1. Fleet Cards

Two fleet cards are available for fueling:

  • Country Mark (Noblesville location only) — for use at the Country Mark station in Noblesville.
  • Circle K — accepted anywhere Circle K operates; a discount applies at Circle K locations.

Your personal gas code is assigned at orientation. If you do not have your code or have forgotten it, contact Teresa or Ben.

When fueling, you will be prompted to enter the current mileage at the pump. Use the odometer reading at the time of fueling.

7.2. End-of-Shift Fueling

Buses must be returned with at least a half tank of fuel. If fueling is needed at the end of your shift, use the nearest available station with the appropriate fleet card and your personal gas code.

7.3. Mid-Shift Fueling

If the fuel level falls below 3/16 of a tank during your shift:

  1. Contact dispatch and get approval to fuel.
  2. Proceed to the nearest available station.
  3. Use the fleet card and your personal gas code.

Do not fuel mid-shift without dispatch approval.

10. Disaster Recovery

10.1. Tablet Failure

If your tablet loses power or stops working mid-shift:

  1. Download Spare Driver from the App Store (iPhone) or Play Store (Android) on your personal phone.
  2. Log in with your normal Spare credentials.
  3. Continue your route — the app works identically to the tablet.

Contact dispatch to let them know you have switched to your phone.

10.2. Spare System Outage

If Spare goes down entirely, use the Disaster Recovery Driver Interface to view your route manifest:

  1. Go to: https://driver.us.sparedr.com/
  2. Organization Key: hce-janus
  3. Username: your normal Spare login — must be all lowercase
  4. Password: 08250

Once logged in, you can view your upcoming pickups and drop-offs, break times, and scheduled PU/DO times. Tap the blue link on any trip to open Google Maps navigation to that stop.

11. Policies

11.1. Breaks

Drivers receive a 30-minute unpaid lunch break and clock out accordingly. Your route schedule in Spare may also include 20-minute gaps between trips. These gaps are a normal part of route planning and provide built-in flexibility in your schedule. Use the time as available — trips may be assigned to fill gaps depending on service demand. Dispatch will notify you of any changes to your schedule.

11.2. Idling

When the outside temperature is between 45°F and 75°F, do not idle the vehicle for longer than 15 minutes at a time. Be aware of high-idling periods in your shift, such as after your pre-trip or during lunch, and minimize idling during those times.

Issued November 4, 2025

11.3. Dress Code

Company uniforms are no longer issued to new employees. The following dress code applies to all drivers:

  • Company shirts previously issued, or any Janus-branded clothing, may be worn.
  • Collared shirts and t-shirts are permitted, provided a professional and neutral appearance is maintained.
  • Khaki, cargo-style pants, or jeans are permitted.
  • Shorts are permitted year-round, provided they do not interfere with completing your shift.
  • Shoes, sneakers, and boots are permitted. No open-toed shoes.
  • Hats may be worn if they do not obstruct the driver's view or interfere with vehicle operation.
  • All employees are expected to maintain good hygiene and be neat in appearance.
  • Attire containing profanity, vulgarity, or politically charged messaging is strictly prohibited.

Issued February 12, 2026

11.4. Time Off Requests

Most trips are assigned to routes beginning 8 days before the scheduled day of service. Requests for time off within that 8-day window are more difficult to approve and can disrupt service to riders.

  • Provide as much advance notice as possible when requesting time off.
  • If your request falls within the 8-day window, contact the manager on duty in addition to submitting the request in Accupay.

Issued November 4, 2025
